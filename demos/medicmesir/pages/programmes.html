Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is studying medicine abroad recognised in Malaysia?
Yes — all universities recommended by MedicMesir are fully recognised by the Malaysian Medical Council (MMC), Malaysian Dental Council (MDC), or Pharmacy Board of Malaysia (BPM). We only recommend universities with this recognition.
What are the costs involved in studying medicine abroad?
Costs vary by country and university. Egypt and Indonesia are generally the most affordable, while Russia and Jordan are mid-range. Our counsellors will provide a full cost breakdown during your consultation, including tuition, accommodation, and living expenses.
Can I apply with SPM results only?
Yes, many of our partner universities accept direct SPM entry, provided you meet their minimum requirements (typically 5Bs in science subjects). Some universities may require a Foundation programme first.
What happens after I graduate? Will I get a job in Malaysia?
All MM graduates to date have received placement in Malaysia for housemanship. MedicMesir provides post-graduation support including housemanship guidance, document verification, and MMC registration assistance. Our 100% placement record speaks for itself.
How long does the application process take?
From consultation to receiving your offer letter, the process typically takes 4–8 weeks depending on the university and intake period. We handle most of the paperwork to make it as smooth as possible for you.
